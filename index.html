    Exercise & Inactivity

    With only about 20% of the United States getting sufficient weekly exercise, the nation as a whole is relatively inactive. This is problematic as physical inactivity can have many negative impacts such as obesity and heart disease.

    MORE STATS

    The Obesity Epidemic

    Obesity has been rapidly rising in the United States. In fact, with more than 1 in 3 adult Americans considered obese—marking the United States the 12th most obese nation in the world—experts have labeled this alarming trend as an epidemic. And with obesity linked to numerous detrimental side effects, such as cardiovascular disease and diabetes, the nation is desperately in need of a solution.

    MORE STATS

    Heart Disease: The Number One Killer

    As the number one cause of death in the United States, heart disease has become a huge problem. Compounded with the lack of exercise exhibited throughout the populus, this problem is set to only get worse. In fact, heart disease costs $320 billion in total medical costs every year.

    MORE STATS

    The Lack of Healthcare Coverage

    And while more people than ever are suffering from obesity and heart disease, 28.2 million Americans are still uninsured, with 45% of them citing the high cost as the number one reason for not enrolling. This means that millions of people are unable to obtain the proper medical care when they need it the most.

    MORE STATS

    There Exists a Solution...

    In the face of these unfortunate trends, we here at Fitness For All believe that there exists a possible solution that will encourage more people to exercise—thereby helping to reduce obesity and heart disease rates, among other diseases—while also making health insurance more affordable for millions.
